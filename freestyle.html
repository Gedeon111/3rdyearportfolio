Entry #7: Freestyle

Some facts about the F22-Raptor

5th Generation Fighter

F-22 RaptorTHE GOAT

FREESTYLE

Many of yall didnt know this … the F-22 Raptor is a 5th-generation fighter jet, which basically means it was built to fight wars before the enemy even realizes one started. Designed and built by Lockheed Martin for the U.S. Air Force, this jet is pure overkill and that's exactly why it fascinates me. The design is the first giveaway that this thing plays a different game. Every sharp angle, flat surface, and weird-looking curve exists for stealth. The F-22 doesn't carry weapons on the outside. Everything is hidden inside to keep its radar signature tiny. On enemy radar, it doesn't look like a fighter jet it looks more like a bird… or a bug… or a radar glitch. Imagine spending billions on air defense just to get clapped by what looks like a flying mosquito. Now here's the twist, despite how agile it is, the F-22 is not meant for dogfighting. Sure, it can do insane maneuvers thanks to thrust vectoring, but its real goal is to erase the enemy before they even know it's there. Using its AN/APG-77 AESA radar, the jet can detect, track, and engage multiple targets from far away while staying almost invisible. Powering all this are two Pratt & Whitney F119 engines, letting the F-22 supercruise at around Mach 1.8 without afterburners and hit speeds up to Mach 2.25. Fast, quiet, and deadly. Fun facts? It has a perfect combat record, was never exported, and production stopped early because it was too advanced and too expensive. Honestly, what fascinates me most is how confident this jet is. It's not here to fight fair it's here to end the fight before it starts. This is exactly why it's THE GOAT.
