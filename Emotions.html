Entry #3: Emotions

Five words, five moments, one story

A Spectrum of Feelings

Life throws unexpected moments at us. Here are five emotions I experienced during one unforgettable encounter on the streets of Zurich.

01 Offended

I felt deeply offended when I was walking to lunch from TBZ, and some random guy on a bicycle screamed "JUGO" at me. It was such a sudden, unprovoked attack on my identity — a word meant to demean, thrown carelessly by a stranger who didn't know anything about me.

02 Astonished

I was astonished that someone would have the audacity to shout something like that in the middle of streets filled with people from all backgrounds. In broad daylight, surrounded by migrants and locals alike — he just didn't care. The boldness of his ignorance left me speechless.

03 Bewildered

What bewildered me most was his complete lack of awareness. Did he not realize how many people around him might be affected by such words? Did he not consider the impact of his actions? I couldn't understand the thought process — or lack thereof — behind such behaviour.

04 Delighted

He, on the other hand, seemed absolutely delighted with himself. As he cycled away, there was this smug satisfaction on his face — like he'd accomplished something clever. His twisted sense of joy at causing discomfort was almost as disturbing as the slur itself.

05 Gobsmacked

In the end, I was left completely gobsmacked. Standing there on the pavement, processing what had just happened. It wasn't fear or anger that hit me first — it was pure disbelief. In 2025, in Zurich, this still happens. That realization stayed with me long after he disappeared from view.

Reflection

This single incident taught me how quickly emotions can cascade from one to another. What started as offence transformed through astonishment, bewilderment, and observation, finally settling into a profound sense of disbelief. These moments remind us that our emotional landscape is rarely simple — it's a spectrum, constantly shifting with our experiences.
